Research

We investigate the molecular mechanisms by which DNA damage in neurons drives neurodegeneration — and how these pathways can be targeted therapeutically.

Research Areas

Five interconnected research programs spanning DNA repair, protein modification, and neurological disease.

01

PARP1–TRIM44–MRN Axis

The PARP1-TRIM44-MRN loop dictates the response to PARP inhibitors — a key pathway in DNA damage signaling and therapeutic targeting in neurodegeneration. We investigate how PARP1 activation recruits TRIM44 to stabilize the MRN complex at DNA double-strand breaks, and how disruption of this axis can be exploited to selectively eliminate neurodegeneration-prone cells or sensitize tumors to PARP inhibitors.

02

Liquid–Liquid Phase Separation & DNA Repair

USP39 promotes NHEJ repair by poly(ADP-ribose)-induced liquid demixing. We study how biomolecular condensates regulate genome integrity in neurons. Emerging evidence shows that DNA repair factors form dynamic condensates at sites of damage; our lab maps the PAR-driven assembly and dissolution of these condensates and their functional importance for genome stability in post-mitotic neurons.

03

LC3B & Transcription-Associated Repair

LC3B drives transcription-associated homologous recombination via direct interaction with R-loops — linking autophagy machinery to DNA repair. This unexpected connection reveals that LC3B, classically known for its role in autophagosome formation, has a nuclear function in resolving transcription-replication conflicts that generate R-loops and threaten genome stability in highly transcribed neuronal genes.

04

Ubiquitin & Ubiquitin-Like Modifiers

Ubiquitin, NEDD8, SUMOylation, FAT10 and other ubiquitin-like modifiers regulate Neuronal Genomic Instability. We map these networks using protein microarrays and high-throughput screening. Our goal is to produce a comprehensive atlas of ULM-regulated DNA damage response proteins and identify novel E3 ligase–substrate pairs as potential drug targets in neurodegeneration and cancer.

05

Neurodegeneration & Therapeutic Translation

Unresolved DNA damage in post-mitotic neurons drives Parkinson's, Alzheimer's and other neurodegenerative diseases. We identify early biomarkers and drug targets. Working with cell and animal models of PD and AD, we characterize DNA damage signatures that precede neuronal loss, and develop small-molecule and peptide-based strategies — including collaboration with APROTBIO and NeuroTAC — to intervene early in disease progression.
